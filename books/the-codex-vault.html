About this book

At Grey Ravel, archaeologist Tessa Sael expects a difficult site, not a dangerous one.

What she finds in the stone behaves less like inscription than operation. The wall is not telling a story. It is organising one.

Before the discovery can be properly understood, the machinery of control begins to close around it. Witness protocols appear too quickly. Access narrows. Terminology itself becomes contested ground.

With local marshal Rovin Hal and the newly arrived James Hale beside her, Tessa is forced into a struggle not only to protect the material, but to protect sequence, language, and meaning.

Because if Grey Ravel is what it appears to be, it does more than reveal a buried archive. It challenges who has the right to certify the beginning.
