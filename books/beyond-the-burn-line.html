About this book

Martin Hale works the outer expansion line, where handled routes, relay stations, and proving windows are the difference between progress and death.

After a retrieval goes wrong, he begins to see the pattern: timings that do not match the ground, reports that grow cleaner as events grow worse, and officials who want memory brought into line.

He trusts the ground, the machinery, and the judgement of the people beside him. He does not trust slogans, and he has no use for tidy language when the facts are not tidy.

What begins as one bad incident becomes something larger. In a place built on procedure, Martin's private notes become dangerous simply because they remain exact.

As the district pushes farther west, Martin is forced to decide what can still be kept, what can safely be said, and what a decent man owes the truth when the system around him prefers a smoother version.
