About this book

Rafe Harker makes his living in the parts of London most people never see: service roads, loading bays, back stairs, side doors. He knows how the city really works. He knows how to get into places he does not belong.

So when an anonymous clip shows his missing brother Jonah alive inside a discreet private clinic called NeedleGate, Rafe does not wait for permission. He bluffs, lies, and forces his way inside.

What he finds is worse than a straightforward crime. NeedleGate is polished, expensive, and outwardly legitimate. Patients are treated. Families visit. Staff do real work. Beneath the calm upper floors, another system operates through hidden routes, restricted transfers, buried archives, and a colder idea of recovery built on privacy, control, and reduction.

Jonah is not simply trapped inside it. He may have chosen it. As Rafe pushes deeper into the building, the question changes from who has taken his brother to what his brother is trying to escape, and whether love becomes dangerous when it refuses to let go.
