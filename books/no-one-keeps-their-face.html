About this book

He wakes in the wrong hotel room with blood on his cuff, a cracked phone, and a photograph of a woman he cannot explain.

Then a broken voice message gives him one instruction: check the blue. The clue leads to a leather folio, altered logs, missing footage, and a quiet system built to turn panic into paperwork. Rooms are renamed. Times are moved. Witnesses are recycled. Every official version feels designed to make the truth disappear.

The deeper he digs, the clearer it becomes that he was chosen for one reason: he was useful. Powerful people close ranks, a survivor keeps pushing him towards what really happened, and an elegant donor network works to keep everything respectable from the outside.

To keep going means deciding what kind of man he is. The tidy lie could save him. The truth could destroy far more than his own life.
